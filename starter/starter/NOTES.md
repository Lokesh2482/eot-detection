# Notes

**Signal used:** The model relies on four families of causal acoustic features computed over multiple trailing windows (0.1–1.5s) before each pause: energy trajectory (level, slope, and recent silence duration), speaker-relative pitch (F0 slope and deviation from the speaker's own recent pitch baseline, in semitones), voicing continuity, and spectral shape (centroid slope, flux, flatness, zero-crossing rate). The top features by impurity importance are the duration of the last active-speech run, F0 slope over the trailing 0.5–1.5s, and spectral flux — consistent with end-of-turn being signaled by falling pitch, decaying energy, and a stabilizing spectrum, not just silence.

**Where it fails:** OOF accuracy is ~60–65%, well above chance but leaving real error. The weakest features are `context_s`, `context_is_short`, and `f0_reference_available`, meaning pauses with too little preceding audio for a reliable speaker baseline are handled worse — likely the model's main blind spot (e.g. very early pauses in short turns). AUC is noticeably higher for Hindi-only training (0.718) than the combined model uses for the actual submission (0.659 on Hindi), suggesting some language-specific prosodic patterns aren't fully shared across English and Hindi, even though the combined model still wins on the operational metric.

(2) Try a per-language ensemble with a shared feature space but blended predictions, to combine the Hindi-only model's better AUC with the combined model's data volume. (3) Prune the three near-zero-importance features and re-validate. (4) Calibrate probabilities (e.g. isotonic regression) since the scorer is threshold-sensitive.
